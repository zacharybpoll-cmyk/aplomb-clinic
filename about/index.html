About Aplomb.

A line built where it should already exist.

Aplomb. is a five-product line for the side effects nobody on the GLP-1 wave is selling you a solution to.

Founder.

Aplomb. was founded by Zachary Poll, a biotech investor who has spent the last decade investing and innovating in healthcare.

Before Aplomb., Zachary was Head of US Healthcare Partnerships at Plug and Play, where he worked with the largest pharmaceutical companies in the world on their corporate-innovation needs — running pilots, evaluating early-stage technologies, and building the deal flow between pharma and the digital-health and biotech startup ecosystem.

He was a clinical researcher at the University of Chicago Medical Center and the Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia. He is a graduate of the University of Arizona Honors College, and holds graduate degrees from the University of Pennsylvania and the University of Cambridge.

Why Aplomb.

Ten years of investing and innovating in healthcare made one pattern obvious: women are still exploited, and they don’t get the care they deserve.

The GLP-1 wave is the latest version of the same pattern. Five million American women are now on Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, or Zepbound. The drug class works — for blood sugar, for weight, for the things the trials measured. The side effects do not get the same attention. The face hollows. The labs drift. The hair sheds. The first month is rough. The breath turns. None of it is in the package insert because none of it is what the trial was powered to detect.

Aplomb. exists to close that gap. One product per side effect, dosed against the strongest evidence available, sold direct without a prescriber gate, designed to sit alongside your existing care. No filler-clinic upsell, no compounded peptide loophole, no pharmaceutical company quietly profiting on both ends.

Just the products that should already exist.
