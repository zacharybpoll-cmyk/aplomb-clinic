Biology

Two drivers, not one.

Face

GLP-1 facial change has two drivers.

Volume below, dermal thinning above. The hollowness is fat-pad loss, which a topical cannot reach. The dermis thins as caloric restriction drops the substrate fibroblasts need to make collagen. That layer is where the Serum works.

Hair

Telogen effluvium follows rapid loss.

The body reads rapid loss as stress and shifts follicles to rest. The shed begins around month four. Iron, vitamin D, and zinc address what triggered it. Not biotin.

Nausea

Delayed gastric emptying.

Forty-four percent report nausea, worst in the titration window. Ginger at one gram daily and B6 are the adjacent-population evidence base. Most quit before the dose works.

Breath

Hyposalivation, ketosis, gastric stasis.

Three converging mechanisms produce volatile sulfur compounds. Sugar-free mints address none of them. Zinc acetate binds them; xylitol shifts the oral microbiome.

Hair · incidence

Why iron, D, zinc, not biotin

Shah et al., 2024, JAAD: EHR cohort, 187,400 GLP-1 users matched to controls, adjusted odds ratio 1.76 for telogen effluvium. Famenini et al., 2014, JAMA Dermatology. FDA 2017: biotin interferes with thyroid assays, so we leave it out on purpose.

Nausea · RCT

Adjacent-population evidence

Wilding et al., STEP-1, 2021, NEJM: 44% report nausea, median episode eight days. Cochrane Review 2014: ginger for nausea, 26 RCTs, 30–50% reduction at one gram per day. Matthews et al., 2015: B6 for nausea.

Breath · mechanism

Three converging pathways
