## What this memo does and doesn't do

The independent mitigation analysis (`Aplomb_mitigation_analysis_v1.docx`) scored Vox Nutrition's "Hair, Skin & Nails Capsule" at **46/100** against GLP-1-induced telogen effluvium. Three load-bearing problems:

1. **Wrong active ingredient.** Biotin only has documented efficacy in already-deficient subjects. Patel 2017's review found every responder in the published case literature had a documented underlying pathology (acquired or inherited biotin deficiency, brittle nail syndrome, uncombable hair) — there is no evidence supporting biotin supplementation in healthy individuals. Durusu Turkoglu 2024's case-control study (N=90 chronic-TE females + 90 controls) confirmed serum and urine biotin levels do not differ between TE patients and controls.
2. **Misses the real deficits.** GLP-1 telogen effluvium is driven by sub-clinical iron / zinc / vitamin D / B12 deficiencies plus protein/caloric insufficiency (broader TE dermatology literature; Durusu Turkoglu 2024 specifically confirmed lower zinc in chronic-TE patients but found no significant differences in iron/ferritin, B12, or vitamin D in their cohort vs. controls — so the iron/D/B12 driver claim depends on the GLP-1-specific mechanism of rapid weight loss + reduced absorption, not the chronic-TE cohort). The Vox capsule does not address any of these at therapeutic dose.
3. **Lab-assay-interference safety risk.** Biotin >2.5 mg/day interferes with ~59% of common immunoassays — TSH, free T4, troponin, hCG. Clinically consequential in a population monitored for thyroid function during rapid weight loss.

This memo finds form-factor-agnostic alternates that Aplomb. can take to RFQ this week.

**Honest reality check before the shortlist.** After three parallel research lanes plus targeted direct fetches, the supplier landscape looks like this:

- **The hair-supplement category is structurally biotin-led.** Most stock "hair vitamin" formulas — including Supliful's flagship Hair, Skin & Nails Essentials Capsule — carry the same 5 mg biotin payload as Vox and inherit the same 2.5 mg assay-interference liability. Buying a different brand of the same formula is not an upgrade.
- **Sub-100 MOQ on a meaningfully better formulation is rare.** The two paths that clear the 100-unit gate are (a) print-on-demand topical serums (Supliful, no biotin systemic risk, but rosemary/peptide concentrations not disclosed so we can't verify Panahi-2015-grade dosing), and (b) one collagen+iron+zinc+D capsule trial at Custom Nutra (48 units, $84). Everything else is 1K–10K MOQ.
- **The highest-scoring candidate (Lief Labs + Rousselot Peptan, 78/100, +32 vs. Vox) requires a 10K MOQ commit (~$10K).** It's a scale-up tier, not a launch tier. Honesty about this matters because the scoring rubric rewards mechanistic alignment, and Lief+Peptan is the only formulation that covers all four GLP-1 TE drivers at near-therapeutic doses.

The 7 candidates below cover three operational tiers — print-on-demand launch (Supliful), low-MOQ trial (Custom Nutra, FormuNova), and scale-up (Lief Labs, Vox upgrade). Each has a verified URL, an honest score against the same 7-dimension rubric used in the mitigation analysis, and a clear "next step." Several score below the Vox 46 baseline; that's noted explicitly so the tradeoffs are visible.
