## What this memo does and doesn't do

BLIS Technologies (NZ) is non-responsive on email. Probi (their licensed US distributor) confirmed a **300,000-unit MOQ** on lozenges — operationally impossible for an MVP. This memo finds form-factor-agnostic alternates that Aplomb. can take to RFQ this week.

**Honest reality check before the shortlist.** After three parallel research lanes plus targeted direct fetches, here's what the supplier landscape actually looks like:

- **Sub-100 MOQ on a stock-formula K12 lozenge does not exist as a publicly-catalogued product.** The K12 strain's commercial structure (BLIS-NZ → Probi → finished-goods CMOs) gates it behind RFQ relationships at every level.
- **Most white-label supplement CMOs do not publish MOQ or pricing on their websites.** Sourcing happens via sales conversation, not catalog browsing. We have to RFQ to confirm both MOQ and price for almost every candidate.
- **What does exist with verified product pages**: (a) print-on-demand stock formulas with 1-unit MOQ but with non-K12 active stacks, (b) finished consumer brands with wholesale (not strict white-label) programs, and (c) Asia-sourced PL-OEM listings with sub-100 MOQ that need quality-assurance verification.

The 7 candidates below cover those three paths. Each has a verified URL, an honest score against the existing 7-dimension rubric, and a clear "next step" — RFQ contact, wholesale application, or sample order. Some score below the BLIS K12 baseline of 71/100; that's noted explicitly so the tradeoffs are visible.

## Methodology note

Same 7-dimension rubric as `Aplomb_mitigation_analysis_v1.docx`: Mechanistic match (20), Clinical evidence quality (20), Effect size (15), Dose adequacy (15), Mechanism coverage (10), Tolerability in GLP-1 users (10), Realistic adherence (10). Total 100. Scores are honest and per-dimension grounded — a reader can re-derive each composite from the per-dimension breakdown.

Where MOQ is not publicly disclosed, the candidate is included if (a) the supplier has a published wholesale or PL program and (b) the candidate's score band meaningfully informs the choice. RFQ is required for every candidate except #5 (Alibaba Ningbo Wellon, 30 confirmed) and #6 (Supliful, ~1 via POD). This reflects how the white-label supplement industry actually operates — pricing and MOQ live in sales conversations, not catalog pages.
