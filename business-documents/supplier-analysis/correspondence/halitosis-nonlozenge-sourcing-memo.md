## Executive Summary

A systematic search for private-label / white-label non-lozenge halitosis products meeting AFTER.'s hard gates (MOQ ≤ 100 units OR total cost < $250, verified direct product URLs) **returned zero viable candidates** in the current market.

**Key finding:** Non-lozenge halitosis white-label is a thin market segment. Most oral care suppliers either (a) focus on toothpaste/rinse consumer brands with no published B2B catalogs, (b) manufacture lozenges only, or (c) serve custom formulation at 500+ unit MOQs.

**Recommendation:** Either (1) stay with BLIS K12 lozenge (current lead, score 82/100), (2) contact CMO suppliers directly for pricing on low-MOQ custom formulation, or (3) negotiate a sample program with format-shifter CMOs.

## Why Format-Shifters Are Sparse at Low MOQs

1. **Evidence hierarchy problem:** Lozenges (especially zinc-based) have 40+ years of clinical validation. Non-lozenge formats (sprays, strips, gels) trade some evidence depth for convenience/novelty. Manufacturers know this and price accordingly.

2. **Tooling economics:** A new lozenge die costs $50K+; an existing die amortized over thousands of units = low per-unit cost. Oral sprays require bottle tooling; strips need form-fill-seal machinery. These are higher capex per unit at small batch sizes.

3. **White-label is a B2B segment:** Most CMOs stock 200–1000 unit MOQs for white-label because they print custom labels in batches. MOQ ≤ 100 on a non-standard format is a cost exception, not the rule.

4. **Halitosis is a niche within oral care:** Most private-label suppliers focus on toothpaste, mouthrinse, and general freshening. Halitosis-specific blends (targeting VSC production + hyposalivation + acetone) are customized, not stock formulas.

## Options Going Forward

### Option A: Stay with BLIS K12 Lozenge (Baseline)
- **Lead supplier:** BLIS Technologies (Redmond, WA — licensed US distributor, manufacturer).
- **Score:** 82/100 (clinical evidence for zinc + chlorhexidine on VSC reduction; high brand recognition).
- **MOQ:** 100 units.
- **Cost:** $200 total ($2/unit).
- **Form-factor advantage:** Highest evidence, proven delivery, longest shelf life.
- **Form-factor disadvantage:** Lozenge format may have lower adherence/appeal in novelty-seeking market segment.

### Option B: Direct CMO Outreach (Custom Formulation)
Contact CMOs with confirmed non-lozenge capability:
1. **SCN BestCo** (gels, chews, sprays, strips) — request halitosis-specific formula RFQ.
2. **Liquid Manufacturing** (sprays, rinses) — request custom acetone-counteracting spray formulation.

**Trade-off:** Likely MOQ 500–1000 units ($3,000–5,000 total), 8–12 week lead time, higher technical risk (formulation must be validated).

### Option C: Negotiate Sample Program
Propose a 3-month pilot: 50 units of a format-shifter at a higher per-unit cost (~$4–5) to test market demand before committing to full MOQ.
- **Feasibility:** Depends on CMO willingness; most will negotiate sample runs if relationship looks long-term.
- **Risk:** Margins compressed; per-unit costs won't hit $2 until you hit 500+ MOQ.

### Option D: White-Label Existing Brands' Formulas
Identify if **TheraBreath** (zinc) or **SmartMouth** (activated zinc) have licensed CMO partners willing to private-label. Requires detective work: contact brand, ask "Who manufactures your product?" and escalate to that CMO.
- **Feasibility:** Medium; brands often refuse to name manufacturers, but corporate registration may reveal it.
- **Advantage:** Leverages existing evidence + brand reputation.

## Recommendation

**For MVP launch (Month 1):** Ship BLIS K12 lozenge as the halitosis SKU. Evidence is strongest, MOQ is achievable, cost is low.

**For Month 2–3 optimization:** Simultaneously run Option B outreach to SCN BestCo and Liquid Manufacturing. Request pricing on 100-unit trial batch for a custom format-shifter (oral spray, gel, or strip). If pricing is under $400 total, allocate for Form-Factor 2 testing.

**For Month 4+ portfolio expansion:** If market testing shows format-shifter demand exceeds lozenge, commit to the higher MOQ (500–1000 units) on the winning format.

This sequencing preserves capital, de-risks the launch, and keeps the option value open.
