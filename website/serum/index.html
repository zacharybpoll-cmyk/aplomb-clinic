What it is.

A water-based serum in a 30 mL frosted-glass bottle. Seven active clinical ingredients — three peptides (Matrixyl 3000 and Matrixyl Synthe’6) plus four plant actives (Centella, Kakadu Plum, Bearberry, Licorice) and a low-molecular-weight hyaluronic acid carrier. One to two drops to clean skin, morning and evening, applied to mid-face, jawline, and neck. Sits cleanly under sunscreen, makeup, or any moisturizer you already use.

Built for women on a GLP-1 (or coming off one) who are seeing the dermal signature of rapid weight loss in the mirror — the texture change, the deepening fine lines, the crepey quality that makes the layer above the cheekbone read older than the rest. Cosmeceutical trials of these peptides measure changes at 8 to 12 weeks of consistent twice-daily use.

What it works on.

Rapid weight loss on a GLP-1 changes the face two different ways. The visible hollowness comes from subcutaneous fat-pad loss — fifty to one hundred millilitres of fat-compartment volume disappears in the first ninety days as the body draws down its stores (Cotofana 2021). A topical can’t reach that layer; fillers and biostimulators do.

The second, quieter change is dermal. Caloric restriction drops the protein and cofactor supply that fibroblasts need to maintain collagen, and the dermis itself thins. Skin texture changes; fine-line depth deepens; the appearance of the hollows is amplified by the layer above. Mechanistically the same dermal aging seen in any rapid-weight-loss population, compressed into nine to sixteen weeks. That is the layer where this serum works.

What it will not do.

It will not restore facial volume. For the hollows themselves, the right call is a board-certified dermatologist about HA fillers, Sculptra, or fat grafting. Frank, the NYC dermatologist who named the pattern, puts the average reflation spend at $5,000 once the volume is gone — that is the volumetric half. This serum is for the layer above.

If you are already exploring fillers for volume, this serum supports the collagen layer that holds the result. It sits beside injectables, not in place of them.

Evidence.

No GLP-1-specific topical trials exist for this category, and you should be skeptical of any topical brand that claims there are. The peptide actives in this serum have evidence in general dermal-aging populations, and we extrapolate to the GLP-1 cohort because the underlying biology — collagen loss from metabolic and nutritional stress — is mechanistically the same.

  • Sederma — Matrixyl 3000
    Up to 45 percent reduction in deep-wrinkle surface area at 2% formulation, twice-daily, 8 weeks; +20 percent skin tonicity by profilometry. Manufacturer-sponsored technical dossier (Sederma SAS).
  • Sederma — Synthe’6
    N=25, ages 42–70, 2 months at 2% formulation. In vitro: collagen I +105%, collagen III +104%, collagen IV +42%.
  • Li Y et al., 2023
    N=37, 12 weeks. +54.99 percent dermal collagen density, +18.81 percent elasticity by ultrasound and cutometer. Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology 22(8):2169–2178. No placebo arm.

How it works.

Three peptides plus four plant actives, each chosen for a specific role on the dermal half:

  • Matrixyl 3000
    Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 + Palmitoyl Tetrapeptide-7. A peptide complex with measured collagen-stimulation effects in cosmeceutical trials.
  • Matrixyl Synthe’6
    Palmitoyl Tripeptide-38. Targets six dermal-matrix proteins. Reduces fine-line depth and smooths surface texture.
  • Centella Asiatica
    Triterpene saponins. Long-standing collagen-synthesis support; the same active in scar-care protocols.
  • Kakadu Plum
    The highest natural source of vitamin C. Antioxidant defence + cofactor for collagen synthesis.
  • Bearberry leaf extract
    Arbutin. Supports tone evenness over the same window the dermal change compresses into.
  • Licorice root
    Glycyrrhizin + glabridin. Anti-inflammatory and brightening, paired with bearberry for a luminous-not-glowing finish.
  • Hyaluronic acid (low-MW)
    The carrier that gets all of the above through the stratum corneum and into the layer where the fibroblasts are.

Routine.

One to two drops to clean skin, AM and PM, layered under moisturizer and SPF. SPF discipline matters more than any peptide.

Pair with adequate dietary protein (≥1.6 g/kg body weight) and vitamin C intake. Fibroblast collagen synthesis is substrate-limited; the topical does its half, your kitchen does the other.

Compatible with retinol or tretinoin on alternating evenings rather than layered. Peptides and retinoids both act on collagen but through different pathways; layering increases irritation without proportional benefit.
